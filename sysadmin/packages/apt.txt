---------
-0- about
---------
apt is short for aptitude and is the package manager for various debian
based distributions.  In general, `apt` is the "old" interface.  The
various `apt-*` are the new interface.  The `apt-*` are more specific
and might offer extra functionality.


-----------
-1- sources
-----------
Apt generally connects to various repositories listed in:
	/etc/apt/sources.list
	/etc/apt/sources.list.d/*

The syntax for these files:
[deb|deb-src] [opt1=val1 opt2=val2 ...] uri suite component1 component2 ...

examples:
	deb http://archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/ jammy main restricted
	deb http://archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/ jammy-updates multiverse
	deb-src http://archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/ jammy-updates multiverse

options:
	arch: amd64, aarch64 ??
	lang:
	target:
	pdiffs
	by-hash
